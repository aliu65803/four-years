# Week 6 Update

This week, `Four Years` expanded from a first-year demo into a full multi-year narrative structure covering Years 2 through 4. The project now supports semester-to-semester continuity, with cumulative stats, relationship tracking, branching scenes, and long-term narrative consequences.

## Year 2: Sophomore Year

Sophomore year focuses on identity, splitting paths, and the tension between old life and new life.

- `Sophomore Fall` centers on growing independence, future pressure, and the introduction of Derek as a slow-burn romantic prospect.
- `Sophomore Spring` deepens the emotional conflict between maintaining hometown friendships and investing in the new life built on campus.
- Mina's role was refined into a best-friend and college-anchor figure rather than a messy romantic ambiguity.
- Professor Alden continues as a mentor-shaped academic influence.
- Branching scenes now depend on carryover stats, Mina/Alden relationship levels, and whether the player invested in work, friendship, or stability.

## Year 3: Junior Year

Junior year shifts into ambition, burnout, and future anxiety.

- `Junior Fall` explores velocity: mentorship, recommendation letters, internship pressure, and the difficulty of balancing a real relationship with Derek against academic and career momentum.
- `Junior Spring` focuses on consequence: burnout, offer emails, career fairs, relationship strain, and the realization that adulthood is already beginning.
- Derek becomes a real boyfriend path, with scenes focused on emotional openness, scheduling, and support under stress.
- Mina becomes the emotional anchor who helps the player stay grounded while life accelerates.
- Junior-year branching includes burnout-sensitive scenes depending on mental health.

## Year 4: Senior Year

Senior year focuses on closure, commitment, and life after graduation.

- `Senior Fall` explores leadership, handoff, mentorship closure with Alden, and the emotional strangeness of almost leaving campus.
- `Senior Spring` centers on endings, graduation, future planning, and deciding what relationships and values carry forward into adulthood.
- Derek's storyline evolves into post-grad future conversations and a tested long-term relationship path.
- Mina's storyline becomes one of gratitude, memory, and chosen friendship as a lasting form of home.
- The player now reaches a full 8-semester arc ending in graduation and a final reflective outcome shaped by cumulative choices.

## Systems and Story Progression Added

Alongside the scenario writing, the game's narrative systems were extended to support:

- multi-semester progression across all four years
- cumulative stat carryover
- relationship tracking for Mina, Derek, Professor Alden, and hometown friends
- scene unlocking and variation based on prior choices and relationship totals
- continued map/location tracking across semester scenes
- semester-specific reflections through sophomore, junior, and senior year
